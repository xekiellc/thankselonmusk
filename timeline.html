From Pretoria to Mars

THE ELON MUSK TIMELINE

Every founding, launch, milestone, and moonshot — from a kid teaching himself to code to the most consequential builder in human history.

1971
Origins
Born in Pretoria, South Africa
Elon Reeve Musk born June 28, 1971. Shows early aptitude for reading and computers.
1983
Early Life
Sells First Software at Age 12
Self-taught on a Commodore computer, Elon creates and sells a video game called Blastar for $500.
1992
Early Life
University of Pennsylvania
Earns dual degrees in economics and physics. Begins PhD at Stanford — leaves after two days to start companies.
1995
Zip2
Co-founds Zip2
First company — web software for newspapers. Sold to Compaq for $307M in 1999. Elon's share: $22M.
1999
X.com / PayPal
Founds X.com — Becomes PayPal
Online financial services company merges with Confinity to become PayPal. eBay acquires it for $1.5B. Elon nets $180M.
2002
SpaceX
Founds Space Exploration Technologies
Invests $100M of personal fortune. Mission: make humanity multi-planetary. Industry experts give it near-zero odds.
2004
Tesla
Joins Tesla Motors as Chairman
Leads Series A funding of $7.5M. Takes over as CEO in 2008. Mission: accelerate sustainable energy transition.
2008
SpaceX
Falcon 1 Reaches Orbit
After three failures nearly bankrupting SpaceX, Falcon 1 becomes the first privately developed liquid-fueled rocket to reach orbit.
2012
Tesla
Model S Launches — Changes Everything
Named Motor Trend Car of the Year. Earns highest Consumer Reports score ever recorded.
2015
SpaceX
Falcon 9 Booster Lands Itself for First Time
Orbital rocket booster returns and lands vertically — declared impossible by aerospace industry. Reusability era begins.
2016
Neuralink
Co-founds Neuralink
Brain-computer interface company aimed at restoring mobility and eventually merging human cognition with AI.
Boring Co
Founds The Boring Company
After tweeting frustration about LA traffic, starts a tunneling company applying first principles to reduce costs 10x.
2018
SpaceX
Falcon Heavy Launches with Roadster
Most powerful rocket since Saturn V carries Elon's Tesla Roadster to Mars orbit with mannequin Starman aboard.
2020
SpaceX
First Private Crewed Mission to the ISS
Crew Dragon launches NASA astronauts Behnken and Hurley. SpaceX becomes first private company to send humans to orbit.
2021
Tesla
Tesla Reaches $1 Trillion Market Cap
Joins the exclusive club of trillion-dollar companies. Elon becomes world's richest person for the first time.
2022
X
Acquires Twitter for $44B — Renames it X
Restores free speech, reinstates banned accounts, cuts costs by 80%, introduces creator monetization and Community Notes.
2023
xAI
Founds xAI — Launches Grok
Creates truth-seeking AI company. Grok integrates into X for hundreds of millions of users. Raises $6B+ in funding.
2024
Neuralink
First Human Neuralink Patient Implanted
Noland Arbaugh, paralyzed from shoulders down, controls a computer cursor with thoughts. Games, browses, communicates independently.
SpaceX
Starship Booster Caught by Mechazilla
After iterative testing, Starship's Super Heavy booster is caught mid-air by the launch tower arms — a world first.
2025
DOGE
Leads Department of Government Efficiency
Applies first-principles thinking to federal government. Identifies $160B+ in savings. Most significant government audit in modern history.
xAI
Grok 3 Released with Advanced Reasoning
Colossus supercomputer — 100,000+ H100 GPUs — powers Grok 3, competing with the world's frontier AI models.
2026 →
SpaceX
Mars Mission on the Horizon
Starship on track for first uncrewed Mars mission. Humanity's multi-planetary future moves from vision to imminent reality.
